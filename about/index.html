About

Welcome. I am Paulo Palacios Valdez and I study Mathematics at the University of Toronto. I intend to pursue a PhD in Mathematics to research something within the conceptual realm of Set Theory or Topology. Although math is my biggest commitment, I enjoy many of the humanities.

Origin

I was born in San Diego, CA and grew up in a Mexican coastal city named Ensenada down the Baja coast. To much of my parents' chagrin (who are software engineers), I fell in love with the much more abstract, less immediately applicable, yet beautiful field that is pure mathematics.

It started when I stumbled upon Fermi problems— named after Italian-American physicist Enrico Fermi. These are estimation puzzles that challenge you to decompose seemingly impossible questions to a series of manageable, educated guesses. The most famous problem is, "How many piano tuners are in Chicago?" which I myself like and implore you to try.

Interests

Over the years I've bounced around many things, but I find that I'm especially fond of boxing, piano and philosophy.

I like to collect fountain pens simply because I think they're very neat devices, and when I travel I keep a postcard of my favorite places.

I enjoy programming a lot but treat it as a tool.

Missions

I actually only started liking math when I encountered proof-based mathematics which was later than I'd like to admit. After I began telling people about it, I often heard them say they could "never do math" and I want to show them otherwise.

But not everything in life is about math. In some distant future, once I've retired, I'd like to make it as an author. I want to learn from others and form as many bonds as my life allows me to. I'd like to perform jazz as my grandfather once did. Perhaps most importantly though, I want to be a stronger, kinder, more gentle person.
